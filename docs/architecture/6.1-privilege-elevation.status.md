## Outstanding / open questions

- `ElevatorConfig` vs the idiomatic `elevator.Config` — `<Domain>Config` family consistency vs Go idiom.
- The per-unit requirement representation: a named-offer reference vs §6's `elevation.Policy` on the unit.
- Provider package location: `pkg/op/provider/elevator/` (matches today's providers) vs a broader move to
  `pkg/provider/` (the latter is a separate, larger decision).

## Discrepancies (design vs. current code)

- The only elevation surface today is the empty no-op `flow.Provider.Elevate()` (`pkg/op/provider/flow/provider.go:507`);
  the mechanism is unbuilt.
- `op.ElevationOffer` / `ElevationStrategy` (`pkg/op/elevation_policy.go`) is plumbed but unconsumed; this design gives
  it a consumer.
- §6 cites `internal/execution/flow/elevate.go` — stale; the live surface is the `flow` provider.
- §6's privileged-process `ElevationProvider{Start,Exec,WriteFile,Stop}` and the token orchestrator share a name today —
  to be disambiguated in the `elevator` package.
